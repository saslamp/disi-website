Introduction
MINI-COURSE ON ONLINE SAFETY FOR KIDS AND TEENS

The internet opens up a wealth of opportunities for children and has become a big part of how they develop, discover, and see the wider world. The use of the internet in a safe and responsible way is pertinent to everyone and most especially kids and teens as it can either have a positive or negative effect on your child’s brain.

General benefits of the use of the internet among kids and teens…

  • Access to more information, knowledge, and learning (for instance educational resources)
  • Enhances communication, connectivity, and sharing
  • Online games can enhance teamwork and creativity
  • Easy access to life-changing opportunities
  • Recent research has shown that households with computers perform better academically
  • Improve both visual intelligence and hand-eye coordination

The overuse of the internet also has its cons…

  • Addiction, time-waster, and causes distractions
  • Affects sleep cycles and could lead to a lack of concentration
  • Access to inappropriate contents
  • Identity theft and Viruses
  • Affects focus and patience
  • Could lead to social isolation

Online safety simply means staying safe while using the internet. It is also the act of maximizing the user’s personal safety and security risks on private information and self-protection from computer crimes.

Personal Information

Personal information is any piece of information that can be used to identify, locate, or contact a person.

Examples of personal information include an individual's:

  1. Full Name
  2. Home and school address
  3. Telephone number
  4. Passport Number
  5. Age, Date of birth
  6. Gender
  7. Email address
  8. Driver’s license number
  9. Social security number
  10. Account number, credit or debit card number
  11. Personal characteristics, including photographic image, fingerprints, handwriting, or other unique biometric data
Stong Password

A password is a secret word or phrase that must be used to gain admission to a place. It is also a string of characters that allows access to a computer system or service.

A strong password is a password that is NOT your name, your nickname, your friend’s name, your family member’s name, your pet name, your favorite quote, your date of birth, or any common name.

A strong password prevents unauthorized access to your electronic accounts and devices and provides essential protection from financial fraud and identity theft.

Characteristics of strong passwords

  • Must contain At least 8 characters, however the more the characters the stronger the password.
  • Must include a mixture of both uppercase and lowercase letters.
  • Must include a mixture of letters and numbers.
  • Must include at least one special character, e.g [. , ! @ # ? ], ensure you do not use < or> in your password, as both can cause problems in Web browsers.

Here are things you should do to stay safe when gaming online

  • Never give away your personal information
  • Never expose your identity
  • Use strong password
  • Install antivirus and keep it up-to-date
  • Respect other game players
  • Report and block any abuser or bully
  • Never meet up with any player
  • Install parental controls
Online Gaming

An online game is a video game that is either partially or primarily played through the Internet or any other computer network available. Online games provide a basis for friends, family, and strangers to interact with each other. With the euphoric feelings received from online gaming, it’s dangers cannot be overlooked. Online gaming poses dangers such as privacy problems, online predators, hidden fees, malware, unwarranted sharing of personal information, cyberbullying, webcam hacks, etc.

Digital Citizenship

Digital citizenship refers to the responsible use of technology by anyone who uses computers, the Internet, and digital devices to engage with society on any level.

Digital Pledge

  1. I will never post or send my personal information to anyone
  2. I will never share my pictures or that of others to anyone without permission from my parents
  3. I will not open emails or click on links sent by people I don’t know.
  4. I will tell my parents if I see any information on the internet that makes me feel uncomfortable
  5. I will not share my password with anyone except my parents
  6. I will never download or install Apps without permission from my parents
  7. I will never send mean or hurtful things to others
  8. I will follow all safety rules set by my parents and teachers
  9. I promise to be safe at all times while on the internet
  10. I promise to be a good digital citizen at all times
Cyber threats, risks and safety measures to take
Cyber bullying

Cyberbullying, online bullying, or cyberharassment is the use of electronic communication to bully a person, typically by sending messages of an intimidating or threatening nature. This kind of activity is most common among teenagers.

Way to deal with a cyberbully

  1. Ignore the person and don’t respond immediately
  2. Don’t retaliate nor reply with a nasty response
  3. Take a screenshot and Inform your parents
  4. Report and block the person
Cyberpedophiles

Cyber pedophiles or online predators are individuals who commit child sexual abuse that begins or takes place on the Internet. Online predators use chat rooms, instant messaging, Internet forums, social networking sites, cell phones, and even video games to lure and groom their victims.

How to stay safe from Cyberpedophile

  1. Ensure not to use photos and names that look/sound suggestive
  2. Avoid anyone who seeks to get too personal
  3. Remember people aren’t always who are say they are on the online
  4. Never ever arrange to meet in person anyone you met online
  5. Be wary of individuals that always flatter you online
  6. Avoid individuals that always seek to get your personal information
  7. Tell your parents or any trusted adult when someone sends you anything that makes you feel uncomfortable
Sexting

Sexting is sending, receiving, or forwarding sexually explicit messages, photographs, or images through the use of any digital device to oneself or to others.

Risk of Sexting

  1. It can put you at risk of blackmail
  2. It can cause damage to your reputation
  3. It can lead to sexual bullying
  4. It can lead to child pornography
  5. It can open doors for Cyberpedophiles
  6. The message never goes away

How to deal with sexting

  1. Do not send, forward, download, screenshot, save any sext
  2. Delete the photo or message immediately
  3. Inform your parents or any trusted adult
  4. If the sext is from a known person, report the person to your parents/trusted adult immediately
Hackers

A hacker is anyone with technical skills and who uses his or her abilities to gain unauthorized access to systems or networks in order to commit crimes.

Ways to avoid hackers

  1. Turn off or logout when you’re done
  2. Choose a strong password
  3. Check your social media security settings
  4. Get two-step verification for your email and other Apps
  5. Use a trusted device
  6. Never share your password with anyone
